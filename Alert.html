Safety Protocol
Emergency Guidelines & Safety Measures

Practical field-tested protocols for each disaster type

1

Immediate Evacuation
Move livestock and stored grain to elevated ground within 2 hours of warning. Do not wait for water to reach your field.

⏱ Do within: 2 hours
2

Protect Harvested Crops
Seal grain bags in waterproof covers. Move stored wheat/rice to rooftops or elevated platforms. Document quantities for insurance claims.

⏱ Do within: 4 hours
3

Drainage & Soil Recovery
After flood recedes, open irrigation channels for rapid drainage. Apply gypsum (CaSO4) to prevent soil crusting. Do not plant for 15–20 days.

📅 Post-flood recovery
💡Tip: Contact NDMA helpline 1700 to register for emergency crop compensation. Keep CNIC and land records ready.
1

Irrigation Adjustment
Increase irrigation frequency to every 3–4 days. Water fields early morning (4–7 AM) or late evening (7–10 PM) to minimize evaporation losses by up to 40%.

🌅 Best time: 4–7 AM daily
2

Crop Heat Stress Management
Apply potassium nitrate (KNO3) foliar spray — reduces heat stress by 25–30%. Provide temporary shade nets over nurseries and vegetable crops.

💊 Spray at dusk
3

Farmer Safety Protocol
No outdoor work between 11 AM – 4 PM. Carry water (2L/hour minimum). Wear light cotton clothing and wide-brim hat. Recognize heat stroke signs: no sweating, confusion, rapid breathing.

⚠ Danger hours: 11 AM–4 PM
🆘Heat stroke emergency: Move victim to shade, wet body with cool water, call 115 (Rescue) immediately.
1

Water Conservation Priority
Switch to drip or furrow irrigation immediately. Implement water rotation schedule among farmers. Prioritize high-value crops (vegetables, cotton) over grain crops.

💧 Target: 50% water reduction
2

Drought-Resistant Crop Switch
Consider sorghum, millet or guar bean as emergency drought-resistant alternatives. Contact Agriculture Department for drought-tolerant seed varieties at subsidized rates.

🌱 Seed subsidy: Call 0800-15000
3

Soil Moisture Retention
Apply mulching (dry crop residue) around plant bases — reduces water loss by 35%. Stop all fertilizer applications — drought-stressed crops cannot absorb nutrients effectively.

📅 Mulch immediately
💡Government aid: Punjab Agriculture Dept drought compensation — register at district office with land records and crop loss estimate.
1

Pre-Storm Field Preparation
Harvest any ripe crops immediately — do not leave standing crops during storm forecast. Tie tall crops (maize, sugarcane) to stakes. Close all irrigation channels to prevent waterlogging.

⏱ Do 24 hrs before storm
2

Equipment & Structure Security
Store all farm equipment in sheltered areas. Secure tube-well motors and electrical equipment. Remove loose materials that could become projectiles in high winds (>60 km/h).

🚜 Secure before winds hit
3

Post-Storm Recovery
Check for standing water and create drainage channels. Survey crop damage within 48 hours and photograph for insurance. Report infrastructure damage to local patwari for official record.

📸 Document everything
⚡Lightning danger: Stay away from trees and water during storms. Move to a concrete building. Call 1122 for emergencies.
1

Evacuation Checklist
Take: CNIC, land records, stored cash, 3-day food supply, medicines, mobile phones (fully charged). Move livestock to evacuation centres — government provides transport assistance.

🎒 Pack in 30 mins max
2

Designated Relief Camps
Go to nearest school, college or government building as designated by district administration. Do NOT return to affected area until official all-clear is given.

🏕 Follow official routes only
3

Communication Protocol
Register with relief camp authorities immediately. Notify family of your location. Use SMS rather than calls to preserve network capacity during emergencies.

📱 SMS saves network capacity
🚨Emergency Numbers: NDMA 1700 · Rescue 1122 · Police 15 · Edhi Foundation 115
